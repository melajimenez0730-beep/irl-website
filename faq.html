Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs, services, and approach to legacy building.

Legacy, in our context, is far more than an inheritance. It encompasses your values, relationships, financial resources, cultural heritage, and the stories that shape your family's identity. Legacy isn't just what you leave behind financially — it's the full spectrum of what you've lived and how you've impacted those around you. IRL exists to help families document, protect, and transmit all of it.

Estate planning addresses the legal and financial transfer of assets — wills, trusts, tax strategy. IRL takes a broader, more holistic approach that includes the intangible aspects of legacy: your stories, values, cultural traditions, and family dynamics. We work alongside legal and financial advisors, not instead of them. Think of us as the relational and governance layer that makes your financial planning actually stick across generations.

No. Legacy is for everyone, regardless of wealth. Every family has valuable stories, traditions, and values worth preserving — and every family benefits from having clearer governance, stronger communication, and shared intention. IRL works with families across economic levels. Our While We're Still Here™ nonprofit arm specifically exists to bring these tools and frameworks to communities with less financial access.

IRL is the platform — it provides education, tools, frameworks, and accessible community programs designed for families at all stages. IRL Advisors is our network of credentialed professionals who offer personalized, high-touch services for families with more complex planning needs. If you're not sure which is right for you, start with the Legacy Snapshot and we'll point you in the right direction.

The best time to begin legacy planning is now — regardless of your age, family status, or wealth level. Legacy isn't something you build at the end of life; it's something you build while you're still here. The earlier a family begins having intentional conversations, aligning values, and building shared systems, the more they have to pass on — and the easier that transition becomes.

We welcome mission-aligned organizations, foundations, wealth management firms, and community institutions to explore partnership. Collaboration can take many forms — workshops, speaking engagements, access to our digital tools, or co-branded resources. Visit our Partnership Pathways page to learn more about how we work with organizational partners, or reach out directly through our contact page.

The best way to support IRL is to engage — take the Legacy Snapshot, share our work with a family you believe in, or explore our programs. You can also support our nonprofit initiative, While We're Still Here™, which brings legacy infrastructure to families who otherwise wouldn't have access. Every conversation you start about legacy in your own family is a form of support too.
